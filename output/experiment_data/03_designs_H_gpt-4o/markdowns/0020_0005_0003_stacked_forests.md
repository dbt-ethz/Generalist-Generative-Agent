## Interpretation  
  
### Implications_form :  
The metaphor of &#x27;Stacked forests&#x27; shapes the building&#x27;s form and massing by evoking a cascading arrangement of volumes that mimic the vertical stratification of a forest. This involves designing a series of terraces or ledges that cascade downwards, each representing a unique ecological layer, from canopy to forest floor. Spatial relationships focus on vertical integration, with spaces that flow seamlessly from one level to another, much like sunlight filtering through the layers of a forest. The geometry emphasizes a balance between open and enclosed spaces, allowing for light and shadow play that adds depth and richness to the experience. The silhouette is envisioned as a stepped form, dynamic in its cascading nature, reminiscent of a forest slope or hillside.  
### Metaphor :  
Stacked forests  
### Key_traits :  
This metaphor suggests a multi-layered, vertical organization resembling a dense, tiered forest. The design would emphasize a sense of hierarchy, depth, and organic growth. It encourages the integration of natural elements, creating spatial richness with varied levels of interaction. The structure would embody vertical connectivity, offering a diverse range of experiences and pathways, much like the layers found in a natural forest ecosystem.  
### Design_task :  
Develop an Architectural Concept Model that embodies the &#x27;Stacked forests&#x27; metaphor by creating a cascading series of terraces or ledges. Each level should represent a different ecological layer, with varying degrees of openness and enclosure. Focus on integrating vertical spaces that connect these layers, allowing for light to penetrate and create a play of shadows. Use a combination of linear and organic shapes to reflect the natural stratification and growth patterns of a forest. Aim for a silhouette that captures the cascading nature of the design, evoking the image of a forest hillside with layers descending in a dynamic, stepped formation.
