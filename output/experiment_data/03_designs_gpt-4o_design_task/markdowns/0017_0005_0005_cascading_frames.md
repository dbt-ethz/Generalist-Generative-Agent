## Interpretation  
  
### Implications_form :  
The metaphor &#x27;Cascading frames&#x27; evokes a building design where structural components are arranged in a sequential, layered manner, suggesting a sense of movement and depth. The massing might consist of a series of frames that appear to descend or ascend in a stepped fashion, creating a dynamic and visually engaging silhouette. This cascading arrangement emphasizes both verticality and lateral connectivity, providing a sense of fluidity throughout the structure. The spatial relationships are likely to be organized to guide the observer through a progression of spaces, with each frame serving as a portal or threshold that connects distinct areas. The geometry could incorporate varying scales and orientations of frames, allowing for a complex interplay of light and shadow that enhances the perception of depth and movement. Additionally, the frames could be designed to create sheltered zones or open terraces, promoting interaction with the environment and enhancing the building&#x27;s contextual response.  
### Metaphor :  
Cascading frames  
### Key_traits :  
This metaphor evokes a sense of dynamic progression and layered depth. It suggests a design where structural elements are organized in successive tiers, creating an interplay of light and shadow. The cascading nature implies fluidity and movement, allowing for visual complexity and spatial continuity. These frames can guide the eye through a sequence of spaces, emphasizing verticality and connectivity within the architecture.  
### Design_task :  
Develop an Architectural Concept Model that captures the &#x27;Cascading frames&#x27; metaphor by constructing a series of frames that vary in scale and orientation, creating a stepped or tiered form. Focus on the interaction between different frames to generate a dynamic visual rhythm, emphasizing both vertical and horizontal continuity. Incorporate a diverse range of materials or finishes to accentuate the layered depth and play of light and shadow. Design the model to guide the observer&#x27;s eye through a sequence of interconnected spaces, with each frame acting as a visual and spatial connector. Explore the potential for these frames to create semi-enclosed outdoor areas or terraces, enhancing the building&#x27;s engagement with its surroundings. Consider integrating pathways or circulation routes within the cascading structure to reinforce the sense of movement and connectivity, while also responding to environmental factors or views.
